    Masala Oats — 1 person lunch

    Type: weekday-dish
    Fridge life: best eaten fresh; same-day fridge only if needed · Freezer-safe: No — oats + potato texture becomes bad
    Active time: 20-25 min (morning) · Makes: 1 filling lunch bowl
    Uses prepped components: none — fresh one-pot dish
    Source: own
    Kitchen-tested: ✅ Yes

    Ingredients

    Ingredient Quantity
    Plain oats ⅔ cup
    Potato 1 small / 80-100 g, small cubes
    Onion 1 small / 50-60 g, chopped
    Tomato 1 small / 60-70 g, chopped
    Green chilli 1, chopped
    Oil or ghee 1.5 tsp
    Jeera ½ tsp
    Hing 1 pinch
    Haldi ¼ tsp
    Red chilli powder ¼ tsp
    Dhania powder ½ tsp
    Garam masala 1 pinch, optional
    Salt ¾ tsp, adjust later
    Water 1¾ cups total
    Lemon juice ½ tsp, optional

    Pressure cooker method — recommended

    This method uses the pressure cooker mainly to cook the potato properly. Add oats after opening so they don't become gluey.

    1. Heat 1.5 tsp oil/ghee in the pressure cooker.
    2. Add jeera + hing. Let jeera crackle.
    3. Add onion + green chilli. Sauté 3-4 min.
    4. Add potato cubes + salt + haldi. Mix well.
    5. Add ½ cup water.
    6. Close cooker. Cook for 1 whistle on medium flame.
    7. Switch off. Let pressure settle naturally for 3-4 min, then open.
    8. Add tomato + red chilli powder + dhania powder.
    9. Cook open for 3-4 min until tomato softens.
    10. Add ⅔ cup oats. Mix/roast for 1 min.
    11. Add remaining 1¼ cups water.
    12. Cook open on low-medium flame for 4-5 min, stirring often.
    13. Add extra splash of water if it gets too thick.
    14. Finish with garam masala pinch and lemon juice.

    Full pressure-cooker shortcut

    Use only if you want a softer, more porridge-like texture.

    1. Follow steps 1-4 above.
    2. Add tomato + spices and cook 2-3 min.
    3. Add ⅔ cup oats + 1¾ cups water.
    4. Mix well, scrape bottom once.
    5. Close cooker and cook on low-medium flame for 1 whistle only.
    6. Switch off immediately.
    7. Let pressure release naturally for 5 min, then open.
    8. Mix well. Add hot water if too thick.

    Notes

    • Prefer the recommended method, not the full shortcut — oats cook fast and taste better when cooked open after the potato is done.
    • Monsoon note: if not eating immediately, cool and refrigerate within 30-40 min as per the plan. Never freeze — the potato rules this out anyway.
