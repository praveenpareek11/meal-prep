# Weekly Variations

*Each variation = one weekend prep session (~1.5-2 hrs) + a 6-day menu, sized for ~10-11 home meals a week. Office-lunch day and outside meals slot anywhere — shift the menu down a day; the freezer absorbs the surplus. Same-day dishes (pongal, pav bhaji, matar paneer, soya bhurji) never appear as multi-day leftovers.*

## Pick your week

| Variation | Character | Protein | Effort | Freezer dependency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | North Indian core | Good | Medium | Low |
| 2 | South Indian lean | Moderate | Low | Medium |
| 3 | Light / gym (sprouts) | Good | Low-medium | Low |
| 4 | High-protein North (soya, paneer) | **Strong** | Medium | Low-medium |
| 5 | Light South/North mixed | Moderate-good | Low | Medium |

## Variation 1 — North Indian Core

**Weekend prep (≈ 1 hr 45 min, parallelised):**

- Pressure cooker rounds: rajma (soaked overnight) → chana → toor dal → potatoes
- Kadai: onion-tomato masala base
- Chop & box: lauki, gobi, onions
- Set curd at night; Sunday shop: veggies + 1 pack idli batter

| Day | Meal (lunch = dinner) | Morning time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Dal tadka + jeera aloo + rice | 25 min |
| Tue | Rajma chawal + curd | 20 min |
| Wed | Idli + sambar-style dal | 30 min |
| Thu | Chole + rice or batch rotis | 25 min |
| Fri | Lauki sabzi (base + lauki box) + rotis + dal (freezer) | 35 min |
| Sat | Gobi-aloo + leftover rotis / or meal out | 25 min |

## Variation 2 — South Indian Lean Week

**Weekend prep (≈ 1.5 hrs):**

- Big batch sambar-style dal (double: 2 cups toor, with lauki + frozen veg)
- Boil: whole moong + potatoes
- Masala base (or use freezer stock from last week)
- Chop & box: beans/gawar, onions
- Buy 2 packs idli batter (pack 2 from Wednesday); set curd

| Day | Meal | Morning time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Idli + sambar-style dal | 15 min |
| Tue | Whole moong curry (base + boiled moong) + rice | 20 min |
| Wed | Veg daliya khichdi (one pot) | 25 min |
| Thu | Idli + sambar-style dal (freezer portion) | 15 min |
| Fri | Beans/gawar sabzi + rice + dal | 35 min |
| Sat | Dosa + aloo masala (boiled potatoes!) — the weekend treat | 45 min |

## Variation 3 — Light / Gym Week

- Soak moong/moth for sprouts (ready Tue) + second sprout batch Wednesday (ready Fri)
- Boil: chana + moong dal + potatoes
- Masala base, curd
- Chop & box: kaddu or lauki, onions

| Day | Meal | Morning time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Moong dal khichdi + curd (one pot) | 20 min |
| Tue | Steamed sprout salad bowl + 2 theplas (batch-made today for the week) | 40 min |
| Wed | Chana chaat (boiled chana + onion + tomato + potato + curd) + thepla | 15 min |
| Thu | Kaddu/lauki sabzi + dal + rice | 30 min |
| Fri | Sprout bowl (batch 2) + thepla + curd | 10 min |
| Sat | Aloo-mangodi curry + rice | 25 min |

## Variation 4 — High-Protein North Indian Week

**Weekend prep (≈ 2 hrs, parallelised):**

- **Saturday night:** soak rajma + chana separately
- Pressure cooker rounds: rajma (5-6 whistles + low 10 min) → chana (5-6) → moong/toor dal (3-4) → potatoes (3)
- Kadai: onion-tomato masala base
- Chop & box: gobi, beans/gawar, lauki, onions
- **Batch theplas (6-7):** knead while cooker rounds run, cook on tawa alongside masala base
- Freezer: extra rajma/chana/dal/base in single-meal portions
- **Paneer:** buy fresh Friday/Saturday for the matar paneer day — it won't last from the weekend shop
- Set curd at night
- **Sprout timeline:** Sunday night soak ½ cup moong/moth → Monday morning drain into covered colander → rinse/drain Mon evening + Tue morning → Tuesday steam 8-10 min before eating. *(If prepping Saturday, start the soak Saturday night.)*

| Day | Meal | Morning time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Rajma chawal + curd | 20 min |
| Tue | Steamed sprout bowl + 2-3 theplas + curd | 15 min |
| Wed | Soya bhurji + rotis | 25 min |
| Thu | Lauki kootu + rice | 25 min |
| Fri | Kadai vegetable + rotis | 30 min |
| Sat | Matar paneer + rotis/rice | 20 min |

**Protein balance:** strong — rajma, steamed sprouts, soya, dal-based kootu, paneer, peas.
**Freezer-dependent days:** Thu (frozen boiled dal); optionally Mon (frozen rajma from an earlier week).

## Variation 5 — Light South/North Mixed Week

**Weekend prep (≈ 2 hrs, parallelised):**

- **Saturday night:** soak chole/chana + moth/moong separately
- Pressure cooker rounds: chole/chana (5-6 whistles) → whole moong/moth (2-3) → toor/moong dal (3-4) → potatoes (3)
- Kadai: onion-tomato masala base
- Chop & box: lauki, gobi, gawar/beans, onions
- Make + portion sambar-style dal: 2 fridge, 2 freezer
- **Batch theplas (6-7):** knead while cooker rounds run, cook on tawa alongside masala base
- Freezer: extra chana/moong/dal/base
- Set curd at night
- **Sprout timeline:** same as Variation 4

| Day | Meal | Morning time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Idli + sambar-style dal | 15 min |
| Tue | Steamed sprout bowl + theplas + curd | 15 min |
| Wed | Dalia pulao | 25 min |
| Thu | Chole + rice or rotis | 25 min |
| Fri | Ven/Kara pongal | 25 min |
| Sat | Pav bhaji + rotis/dosa/fresh pav | 25 min |

**Protein balance:** moderate-good — chole, sprouts, dalia with frozen veg, sambar dal, moong dal in pongal; lighter than Variation 4.
**Freezer-dependent days:** Mon (frozen sambar-dal), Thu (frozen chole), Fri (frozen boiled moong dal).
