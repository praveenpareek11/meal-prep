# Gawar Aloo Lipta Masala Sabji

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** Same day to 1 day; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because the dish contains potato  
**Active time:** 30-35 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: semi-dry gawar and potato sabji with thick masala coating. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Gawar fali | 300-350 g before trimming, cut into 2 cm pieces |
| Potato | 1 medium, about 130-150 g |
| Onion | 1 medium, about 90-100 g, finely chopped |
| Tomato | 1 large, about 120-140 g, finely chopped |
| Garlic | 4 cloves, finely chopped or crushed |
| Green chilli | 1, finely chopped |
| Oil | 1½ tbsp |
| Jeera | ½ tsp |
| Ajwain | ¼ tsp |
| Hing | 1 generous pinch |
| Haldi | ¼ tsp |
| Red chilli powder | ½ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Dhania powder | 1½ tsp |
| Jeera powder | ½ tsp |
| Salt | ¾ tsp, or to taste |
| Water | ⅓ cup, about 80 ml |
| Amchur | ½ tsp |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp |
| Kasuri methi | ½ tsp, optional |

## Process

1. Peel the potato and cut it into small **1-1.5 cm cubes**. Keep the pieces smaller than the gawar so the potato cooks quickly.
2. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in a kadai over **medium heat**.
3. Add **½ tsp jeera** and **¼ tsp ajwain**. Once they crackle, add **1 generous pinch hing**.
4. Add the chopped onion and cook over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, until soft and lightly golden.
5. Add the garlic and green chilli. Sauté for **1 minute**.
6. Add the chopped tomato, **¼ tsp haldi**, **½ tsp red chilli powder**, **1½ tsp dhania powder**, **½ tsp jeera powder**, and **½ tsp salt**.
7. Cook over **medium heat for 5-6 minutes**, stirring and mashing the tomato, until it becomes pulpy and the masala begins leaving oil at the edges.
8. Add the potato cubes. Mix and sauté with the masala over **medium heat for 3 minutes**.
9. Add the chopped gawar and the remaining salt. Mix thoroughly and sauté uncovered for **4-5 minutes**.
10. Add **⅓ cup water** and scrape any masala stuck to the bottom of the kadai.
11. Cover and cook over **low-medium heat for 16-20 minutes**. Stir every **4-5 minutes**.
12. If the mixture becomes completely dry before the potato and gawar are tender, add **1-2 tbsp hot water** at a time.
13. Check the potato with a spoon and taste one thicker gawar piece. Both should be fully tender.
14. Remove the lid and cook over **medium heat for 4-6 minutes**, stirring regularly. Lightly press **2-3 potato cubes** against the side of the kadai and mix them back into the sabji. This thickens the masala and gives the desired lipta texture.
15. Add **½ tsp amchur**, **¼ tsp garam masala**, and optional **½ tsp crushed kasuri methi**.
16. Cook uncovered for another **1-2 minutes**, stirring gently, until the thick masala coats the gawar and potato.
17. Taste and adjust the salt or amchur. The finished sabji should be moist and masala-coated, but should not have loose gravy.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**, one each for lunch and dinner.
- Each portion pairs with **3 rotis**.
- Remove the dinner portion from the hot kadai and allow it to cool for **30-40 minutes**.
- Transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate immediately.
- Because the dish contains potato, consume it by dinner the same day or within **1 day maximum**.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten in a kadai with **1-2 tbsp water**.
- Cover and reheat over **low-medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, stirring once or twice, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not freeze this sabji.
