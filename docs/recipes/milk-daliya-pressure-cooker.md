# Direct Pressure-Cooker Milk Daliya

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** Same day; cook and eat fresh · **Freezer-safe:** No, because milk-based daliya may separate and become grainy after freezing  
**Active time:** 10-12 min · **Makes:** 1 dinner for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: soft, creamy, mildly sweet wheat daliya cooked directly with milk and a little water.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Daliya / broken wheat | ½ cup, about 75-80 g |
| Ghee | 1 tsp |
| Milk | 1½ cups, about 375 ml |
| Water | ½ cup, about 125 ml |
| Sugar | 1-1½ tbsp, adjust to taste |
| Elaichi powder | ⅛ tsp, optional |
| Almonds or cashews | 1 tbsp, chopped, optional |

## Process — Direct Pressure Cooker

1. Use a **3-litre or larger pressure cooker**. The milk and daliya mixture must remain below **half of the cooker's capacity** so the foaming milk does not reach the vent.
2. Heat **1 tsp ghee** directly in the cooker over **low-medium heat**.
3. Add **½ cup daliya** and roast for **4-5 minutes**, stirring continuously, until lightly aromatic. Do not brown it deeply.
4. Reduce to **low heat**. Add **½ cup water** gradually and stir thoroughly, scraping the bottom so no roasted daliya remains stuck.
5. Add **1½ cups milk** and mix well. Do not add the sugar yet, because sugar can increase sticking.
6. Keep the cooker uncovered over **low-medium heat for 2-3 minutes**, stirring once or twice, until the liquid becomes hot but is not vigorously boiling.
7. Stir once from the bottom, close the cooker, and fit the whistle.
8. Cook over **medium-low heat for 1 whistle**. Immediately reduce to the **lowest heat** and cook for another **4 minutes**.
9. Switch off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally. Do not force-release the pressure because the milk may still be foamy.
10. Open carefully and stir thoroughly from the bottom. Add **1-1½ tbsp sugar** and optional **⅛ tsp elaichi powder**.
11. If the daliya is thicker than preferred, add **2-4 tbsp hot milk** and simmer uncovered over **low heat for 1-2 minutes**.
12. Rest for **3 minutes** before serving. Add the chopped nuts, if using.

## Portioning and Storage

- Eat the full quantity fresh as **1 dinner portion**.
- The daliya will thicken as it rests; loosen it with a little hot milk if needed.
- If any portion remains, cool it to warm within **30-40 minutes**, cover, and refrigerate immediately rather than leaving it at room temperature.
- Reheat only the portion being eaten in a small kadai over **low heat**, adding **2-3 tbsp milk** and stirring until piping hot throughout.
- Do not freeze.
