# Aloo Pyaaz Bhindi Sabji

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** Same day to 1 day; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because the dish contains potato  
**Active time:** 30-35 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: dry, lightly masala-coated aloo-pyaaz bhindi. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Bhindi | 200-230 g after trimming, from about 250 g untrimmed |
| Potato | 1 medium, about 140-160 g, cut into 1 cm cubes |
| Onion | 1 medium, about 90-100 g, thinly sliced |
| Green chilli | 1-2, finely chopped |
| Oil | 1½ tbsp |
| Jeera | ½ tsp |
| Hing | 1 pinch |
| Haldi | ¼ tsp |
| Dhania powder | 1 tsp |
| Red chilli powder | ¼ tsp, optional |
| Amchur | ½ tsp |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp, optional |
| Salt | ¾ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Water | 2 tbsp, for partially cooking the potato |

## Process

1. Make sure the bhindi is completely dry. Cut it fresh into approximately **2-2½ cm pieces**. Cut the potato into **1 cm cubes**, thinly slice the onion, and finely chop the green chilli.
2. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in a kadai over **medium heat**. Add **½ tsp jeera** and let it crackle for **15-20 seconds**, then add **1 pinch hing**.
3. Add the potato, **¼ tsp haldi**, and **¼ tsp salt**. Sauté over **medium heat for 2 minutes**.
4. Add **2 tbsp water** around the sides of the kadai. Cover and cook over **low heat for 5-6 minutes**, stirring once halfway through, until the potato is about half to two-thirds cooked.
5. Remove the lid and cook over **medium heat for 1-2 minutes**, until all visible moisture has evaporated.
6. Add the onion and green chilli. Cook over **medium heat for 3-4 minutes**, until the onion softens slightly.
7. Add the chopped bhindi and mix gently. Cook uncovered over **medium heat for 5-6 minutes**, stirring only once or twice so the bhindi does not break or become sticky.
8. Add **1 tsp dhania powder**, optional **¼ tsp red chilli powder**, and the remaining **½ tsp salt**. Mix gently.
9. Continue cooking uncovered over **medium-low heat for 7-9 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until the bhindi is cooked and the potato is tender.
10. Add **½ tsp amchur** and optional **¼ tsp garam masala**. Mix gently and cook for another **1-2 minutes**.
11. Switch off the heat and leave the sabji uncovered for **3-5 minutes** so excess steam escapes before portioning.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**. Serve one portion for lunch with **3 rotis**.
- Transfer the dinner portion to a separate clean container so it is not repeatedly handled.
- Cool the dinner portion to warm within **30-40 minutes**, then cover and refrigerate immediately.
- Consume the refrigerated portion at dinner the same day; the maximum fridge life is **1 day**.
- Reheat only the dinner portion in a kadai over **low-medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until piping hot throughout.
- If the sabji looks dry while reheating, add **1 tsp oil**. Add only **1 tsp water around the kadai edge** if absolutely necessary.
- Do not refrigerate or reheat the portion again. Do not freeze because the dish contains potato.
