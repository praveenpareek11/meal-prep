# Batch Rotis / Theplas

**Type:** weekend-prep  
**Fridge life:** rotis 2-3 days · theplas 3-4 days · **Freezer-safe:** theplas yes (1 week); rotis no  
**Active time:** 30-40 min per batch · **Makes:** 6 rotis or 6-7 theplas  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own, validated  
**Kitchen-tested:** ❌ not yet  

*Never store dough — it greys and sours in a day. Store cooked rotis instead.*

**Rotis (2-day batch):** for 6 rotis, use 210-240 g atta, ½ tsp salt optional, 1 tsp oil optional, and about 130-150 ml water. Knead soft, rest 10-15 min, roll and cook 6 rotis. Smear light ghee only after cooking if desired. Cool on a plate/wire rack for 5-8 min so steam escapes, then stack in a cloth-lined airtight box and fridge. Revive on a hot tawa 30 sec/side — or 🔲 microwave 20 sec wrapped in a slightly damp cloth.

**Theplas (3-4 day batch, better keeper):** for 6-7 theplas, knead 180 g atta + 45 g besan, ¼ cup curd, 1 tbsp oil, ½ tsp salt, ½ tsp ajwain, ¼ tsp haldi, ½ tsp chilli powder, and optional grated lauki. Add water only as needed to make a firm but rollable dough. Roll thin, cook on medium flame with a little oil on both sides till brown spots appear. Cool fully, then stack in an airtight box with paper/cloth between layers. These stay soft 3-4 days in the fridge — the best roti-family option for meal prep. Fridge is the default in monsoon; their room-temp tolerance is for office-day tiffins, not counter storage.

**Lazy-day rule:** if rotis feel like effort, default to rice. Rice is always fresh (1 bowl = ¾ cup rice + 1.5 cups water, pressure cook 2 whistles, zero attention while you cook the sabzi).
