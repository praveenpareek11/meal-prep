# Gawar Mangodi Sabji

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 2-3 days; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because cooked gawar loses texture and releases water after thawing  
**Active time:** 35-40 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: semi-dry gawar sabji with tender moong dal mangodi and light onion-tomato masala. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Gawar fali | 300-350 g before trimming |
| Moong dal mangodi | ⅓ cup, about 45-50 g |
| Onion | 1 small, about 70-80 g, finely chopped |
| Tomato | 1 medium, about 90-100 g, finely chopped |
| Garlic | 4 cloves, finely chopped or crushed |
| Green chilli | 1-2, finely chopped |
| Oil | 2 tbsp, divided |
| Jeera | ½ tsp |
| Hing | 1 generous pinch |
| Haldi | ¼ tsp |
| Red chilli powder | ½ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Dhania powder | 1½ tsp |
| Jeera powder | ½ tsp |
| Salt | ¾ tsp, or to taste |
| Water | 1 cup, about 240 ml |
| Amchur | ½ tsp |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp, optional |

## Process

1. Wash the gawar thoroughly. Remove the stalk ends and pull away any tough strings from the sides.
2. Chop the gawar into approximately **2 cm pieces**.
3. Heat **1 tsp oil** in a kadai over **low-medium heat**.
4. Add **⅓ cup mangodi** and roast for **3-4 minutes**, stirring continuously, until lightly golden and aromatic. Do not let it turn dark brown.
5. Remove the roasted mangodi to a plate and keep it aside.
6. Add the remaining oil to the same kadai and heat over **medium heat**.
7. Add **½ tsp jeera**. Once it crackles, add **1 generous pinch hing**.
8. Add the chopped onion and cook over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, until soft and lightly golden.
9. Add the garlic and green chilli. Sauté for **1 minute**.
10. Add the chopped tomato, **¼ tsp haldi**, **½ tsp red chilli powder**, **1½ tsp dhania powder**, **½ tsp jeera powder**, and **½ tsp salt**.
11. Cook over **medium heat for 5-6 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until the tomato becomes soft and pulpy.
12. Add the chopped gawar and the remaining salt. Mix and sauté over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**.
13. Add the roasted mangodi and mix gently.
14. Add **1 cup water**. Mix well and bring it to a gentle boil over **medium heat**.
15. Cover the kadai and cook over **low-medium heat for 20-25 minutes**.
16. Open and stir gently every **5 minutes**. Avoid vigorous stirring because softened mangodi can break apart.
17. Check the gawar and mangodi. The gawar should be tender, and the mangodi should be completely soft inside without being hard or chalky.
18. If either remains undercooked, add **2-3 tbsp hot water**, cover, and cook for another **5-7 minutes**.
19. Once cooked, remove the lid and cook over **medium heat for 4-6 minutes**, stirring gently, until the extra moisture evaporates and the sabji becomes semi-dry.
20. Add **½ tsp amchur** and optional **¼ tsp garam masala**.
21. Mix gently and cook for another **1 minute**. Taste and adjust the salt or amchur.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**, one each for lunch and dinner.
- Each portion pairs with **3 rotis**.
- Remove the dinner portion from the hot kadai and let it cool for **30-40 minutes**.
- Transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate immediately.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten in a kadai with **2 tbsp water**.
- Cover and reheat over **low-medium heat for 5-6 minutes**, stirring gently once or twice, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not freeze this sabji.
