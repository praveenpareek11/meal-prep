# Homestyle Paneer Pyaaz-Tamatar Masala

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** Same day to 1 day; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because paneer texture deteriorates after freezing  
**Active time:** 25-30 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: soft paneer in a medium-thick homestyle onion-tomato masala. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Paneer | 200 g, cut into about 2 cm cubes |
| Onion | 1 large, about 140-160 g, finely chopped |
| Tomato | 2 medium, about 180-200 g, finely chopped |
| Garlic | 4 cloves, finely chopped or crushed |
| Green chilli | 1, finely chopped |
| Oil | 1½ tbsp |
| Jeera | ½ tsp |
| Haldi | ¼ tsp |
| Kashmiri red chilli powder | ½ tsp |
| Dhania powder | 1 tsp |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp |
| Kasuri methi | ½ tsp, crushed |
| Salt | ¾ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Water | ½ cup, plus 2-3 tbsp if needed |

## Process

1. Cut **200 g paneer** into approximately **2 cm cubes**. If the paneer is firm, soak the cubes in warm water for **5 minutes**, then drain.
2. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in a kadai over **medium heat**. Add **½ tsp jeera** and let it crackle for **15-20 seconds**.
3. Add the chopped onion and cook over **medium heat for 6-8 minutes**, stirring regularly, until soft and light golden.
4. Add the garlic and green chilli. Cook for **45-60 seconds** until the raw garlic smell reduces.
5. Add the chopped tomatoes and **½ tsp salt**. Cook over **medium heat for 5-6 minutes**, stirring and mashing occasionally, until the tomatoes become soft.
6. Add **¼ tsp haldi**, **½ tsp Kashmiri red chilli powder**, and **1 tsp dhania powder**. Cook over **medium-low heat for 3-4 minutes**, stirring regularly, until the masala becomes thick and the oil begins separating slightly.
7. Add **½ cup water** and mix well, scraping up any masala stuck to the kadai. Bring it to a gentle boil, then simmer over **medium-low heat for 3 minutes**.
8. Add the paneer cubes and the remaining **¼ tsp salt**. Mix gently so the paneer does not break.
9. Cover and cook over **low heat for 4 minutes only**. Do not cook the paneer for too long, or it may become rubbery.
10. Switch off the heat. Add **¼ tsp garam masala** and **½ tsp crushed kasuri methi**. Mix gently and rest covered for **5 minutes**.
11. Check the consistency. If the masala is thicker than preferred, mix in **2-3 tbsp hot water** before serving.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**. Serve one portion for lunch with **3 rotis**.
- Transfer the dinner portion to a separate clean container so it is not repeatedly handled.
- Cool the dinner portion to warm within **30-40 minutes**, then cover and refrigerate immediately.
- Consume the refrigerated portion at dinner the same day; the maximum fridge life is **1 day**.
- Reheat only the dinner portion in a kadai with **1-2 tbsp water**, covered over **low-medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, stirring gently once or twice, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not refrigerate or reheat the portion again. Do not freeze.
