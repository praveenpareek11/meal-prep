# Standalone Gawar Masala Sabji

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 2-3 days; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because cooked gawar becomes excessively soft and watery after thawing  
**Active time:** 30-35 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  
**Kitchen-tested:** ❌ not yet  

*Style: semi-dry gawar-only sabji with simple masalas. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal; curd or dal can be served alongside.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient       |                                           Quantity |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------: |
| Gawar fali       |                              450 g before trimming |
| Oil              |                                           1½ tbsp |
| Jeera            |                                             ½ tsp |
| Ajwain           |                                             ¼ tsp |
| Hing             |                                  1 generous pinch |
| Green chilli     |                         1-2, finely chopped |
| Haldi            |                                             ¼ tsp |
| Red chilli powder|                         ½ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Dhania powder    |                                            1½ tsp |
| Jeera powder     |                                             ½ tsp |
| Salt             |                             ¾ tsp, or to taste |
| Water            |                              ¼ cup, about 60 ml |
| Amchur           |                                             ¾ tsp |
| Garam masala     |                                   ¼ tsp, optional |
| Jaggery or sugar | ¼ tsp, optional; balances bitterness |

## Process

1. Wash the gawar fali thoroughly and drain it. Remove the stalk ends and pull away any tough strings along the sides.
2. Chop the gawar into approximately **2 cm pieces**.
3. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in a kadai over **medium heat**.
4. Add **½ tsp jeera** and **¼ tsp ajwain**. Once they crackle, add **1 generous pinch hing**.
5. Add the chopped green chilli and sauté for **20-30 seconds**.
6. Reduce the heat to **low**. Add **¼ tsp haldi**, **½ tsp red chilli powder**, **1½ tsp dhania powder**, and **½ tsp jeera powder**. Stir for only **10-15 seconds** so the spices do not burn.
7. Immediately add the chopped gawar and **¾ tsp salt**. Mix thoroughly so every piece is coated with the oil and masalas.
8. Sauté uncovered over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, stirring frequently.
9. Add **¼ cup water** and mix. Cover the kadai and cook over **low-medium heat for 16-20 minutes**.
10. Open and stir every **4-5 minutes**. If the kadai becomes completely dry before the gawar is tender, add only **1-2 tbsp water** at a time.
11. Check one of the thicker pieces. The gawar should be completely tender without becoming mashed. If it remains fibrous, cover and cook for another **4-5 minutes**.
12. Once tender, remove the lid and cook over **medium heat for 3-5 minutes**, stirring regularly, until all excess moisture evaporates.
13. Add **¾ tsp amchur**, optional **¼ tsp garam masala**, and optional **¼ tsp jaggery or sugar**.
14. Mix and cook uncovered for **1-2 minutes**. Taste and adjust the salt or amchur.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**, one each for lunch and dinner.
- Each portion pairs with **3 rotis**. Since this is a gawar-only sabji, curd or plain dal can be served alongside for a fuller meal.
- Remove the dinner portion from the hot kadai and let it cool for **30-40 minutes**.
- Transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate immediately. Do not leave it at room temperature until evening.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten in a kadai with **1-2 tbsp water**.
- Cover and reheat over **low-medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, stirring once or twice, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not freeze this sabji.
