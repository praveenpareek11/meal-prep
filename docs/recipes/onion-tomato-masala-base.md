# Onion-Tomato Masala Base

**Type:** weekend-prep  
**Fridge life:** 4 days · **Freezer-safe:** yes (3-4 weeks)  
**Active time:** ~40 min (mostly passive stirring) · **Makes:** makes 6-7 single-meal portions  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own, spice-blooming technique from Hebbars Kitchen curry base  
**Kitchen-tested:** ❌ not yet  

*The single highest-ROI item. Every weekday gravy starts from here.*

**Makes:** ~600-700 g = 6-7 single-meal portions | **Time:** ~40 min, mostly passive stirring

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Quantity                                                 |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------: |
| Onions              | 500 g, finely chopped or thinly sliced (5-6 medium)      |
| Tomatoes            | 600 g, chopped or pulsed coarse (6-7 medium)             |
| Ginger-garlic paste | 2 tbsp (or 8 garlic cloves + 1 inch ginger, crushed)     |
| Oil                 | 4 tbsp                                                   |
| Jeera               | 1 tsp                                                    |
| Salt                | 1 tsp, divided                                           |
| Haldi               | 1 tsp                                                    |
| Red chilli powder   | 1.5 tsp                                                  |
| Dhania powder       | 1 tbsp                                                   |
| Water               | 2-3 tbsp, only for blooming dry spices                   |

## Method

1. Heat oil in your biggest kadai. Add jeera and let it splutter.
2. Add onions + ½ tsp salt. Sauté on medium till deep golden — 12-15 min. Don't rush this; the colour is the flavour.
3. Add ginger-garlic paste, sauté 1-2 min till raw smell goes.
4. Lower the flame. Add haldi, chilli, dhania, and 2-3 tbsp water. Stir 30-45 sec so the spices bloom without burning.
5. Add tomatoes and remaining salt. Cook on medium, stirring occasionally, till completely mushy, thick, and oil separates at the edges — 15-18 min.
6. Cool fully.

## Portion & Store

- 2-3 portions (about 100 g / 2 heaped tbsp each) → fridge box, use within 4 days
- Rest → freezer in small 200 ml containers or a silicone cube tray (2 cubes ≈ 1 portion). Keeps 3-4 weeks.
- To use from frozen: drop the frozen block straight into the hot kadai with a splash of water. No defrosting needed.

## What it becomes / variations

- rajma, chole, aloo-mangodi, matar-paneer-style gravies, dal tadka upgrade, veg curry, even quick pav-bhaji-ish mash with boiled potato.
