# Kadai Vegetable

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 2 days · **Freezer-safe:** no  
**Morning active time:** 30 min first time, ~22 min with jarred kadai masala · **Serves:** 1 person, lunch + dinner  
**Uses prepped components:** onion-tomato masala base — 150 g / 4 heaped tbsp; chopped hardy veg box — 450-500 g  
**Source:** Hebbars Kitchen Veg Kadai, adapted for prepped masala base and hardy monsoon veg  

## Ingredients

| Ingredient               | Quantity                 |
| ------------------------ | -----------------------: |
| Onion-tomato masala base | 150 g / 4 heaped tbsp    |
| Chopped gobi             | 2 cups                   |
| Chopped beans or gawar   | 1½ cups                  |
| Frozen peas              | ½ cup                    |
| Oil                      | 2 tsp                    |
| Jeera                    | ½ tsp                    |
| Ginger-garlic paste      | ½ tsp, optional          |
| Haldi                    | ¼ tsp                    |
| Salt                     | ¾ tsp or to taste        |
| Water                    | ½ to ¾ cup               |
| Kasuri methi             | ½ tsp, crushed, optional |
| Garam masala             | ¼ tsp                    |
| Coriander                | 1 tbsp, optional         |

## Ingredients — quick kadai masala

*Tip: make 3x this quantity once (3 tbsp coriander seeds, 6 dry red chillies, 1½ tsp cumin), store in an airtight jar (keeps 3-4 weeks), and skip Method steps 1-2 thereafter — use 2 tsp from the jar.*

| Ingredient      | Quantity        |
| --------------- | --------------: |
| Coriander seeds | 1 tbsp          |
| Dry red chilli  | 2               |
| Cumin seeds     | ½ tsp           |
| Black pepper    | ¼ tsp, optional |

## Method
1. Dry roast coriander seeds, dry red chilli, cumin seeds, and black pepper on low flame for 2-3 min till aromatic.
2. Cool 1 min, then crush coarse in mortar-pestle or mixer pulse mode. Keep aside.
3. Heat oil in kadai. Add jeera and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté 30 sec.
4. Add gobi, beans or gawar, frozen peas, haldi, and salt.
5. Sauté on medium-high for 4-5 min so vegetables get slight colour.
6. Add ½ cup water, cover, and cook till just tender: gobi/beans 8-10 min; gawar 12-15 min.
7. Add onion-tomato masala base and crushed kadai masala.
8. Mix well and cook uncovered 5-7 min till masala coats the vegetables.
9. Add kasuri methi and garam masala. Mix and switch off.
10. Keep texture semi-dry for rotis; add a splash of water if eating with rice.

## Portion & Store
- Split into 2 portions: lunch + dinner.
- Cool 30-40 min, then refrigerate the dinner portion.
- Fridge life: 2 days max in monsoon.
- Do not freeze finished kadai vegetable because chopped cooked veg texture becomes watery.

## What it becomes / variations
- Use only gobi + peas for a fast version, or beans/gawar-heavy version for a drier roti sabzi.
