# Gawar Tomato Jhol Sabji

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 2-3 days; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because cooked gawar becomes excessively soft and watery after thawing  
**Active time:** 35-40 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: tomato-forward, lightly spiced jhol with tender gawar. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Gawar fali | 350-400 g before trimming |
| Onion | 1 small, about 70-80 g, finely chopped |
| Tomatoes | 3 medium, about 250-300 g, finely chopped |
| Garlic | 5 cloves, finely chopped or crushed |
| Green chilli | 1-2, finely chopped |
| Oil | 1½ tbsp |
| Jeera | ½ tsp |
| Hing | 1 generous pinch |
| Haldi | ¼ tsp |
| Red chilli powder | ½ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Dhania powder | 1½ tsp |
| Jeera powder | ½ tsp |
| Salt | ¾-1 tsp, or to taste |
| Water | 1¼ cups, about 300 ml |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp, optional |
| Jaggery or sugar | ¼ tsp, optional; balances bitterness and acidity |

## Process

1. Wash the gawar thoroughly. Remove the stalk ends and pull away any tough strings from the sides.
2. Chop the gawar into approximately **2 cm pieces**.
3. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in a kadai over **medium heat**.
4. Add **½ tsp jeera**. Once it crackles, add **1 generous pinch hing**.
5. Add the chopped onion and cook over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, until soft and lightly golden.
6. Add the garlic and green chilli. Sauté for **1 minute**.
7. Add the chopped tomatoes, **¼ tsp haldi**, **½ tsp red chilli powder**, **1½ tsp dhania powder**, **½ tsp jeera powder**, and **½ tsp salt**.
8. Cook over **medium heat for 8-10 minutes**, stirring and mashing the tomatoes occasionally, until they become pulpy and the masala begins leaving oil at the edges.
9. Add the chopped gawar and the remaining salt. Mix thoroughly and sauté with the tomato masala over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**.
10. Add **1¼ cups water** and mix well.
11. Cover the kadai and cook over **low-medium heat for 20-25 minutes**, stirring every **5 minutes**.
12. Check one of the thicker gawar pieces. It should be completely tender and should not feel stringy or fibrous.
13. If the gawar is still firm, add **2-3 tbsp hot water**, cover, and cook for another **5 minutes**.
14. Once the gawar is cooked, lightly press a few tomato pieces against the side of the kadai to give the jhol a slightly thicker consistency.
15. Simmer uncovered over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**. The gravy should remain thin and spoonable, not completely dry.
16. Add optional **¼ tsp garam masala** and optional **¼ tsp jaggery or sugar**. Mix and cook for **1 minute**.
17. Taste and adjust the salt. Add a small splash of hot water if the gravy has become thicker than desired.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**, one each for lunch and dinner.
- Each portion pairs with **3 rotis**.
- Remove the dinner portion from the hot kadai and let it cool for **30-40 minutes**.
- Transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate immediately. Do not leave it at room temperature until evening.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten in a kadai over **low-medium heat**.
- Add **1-2 tbsp water**, cover, and reheat for **4-5 minutes**, stirring once or twice, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not freeze this sabji.
