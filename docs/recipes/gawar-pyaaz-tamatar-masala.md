# Gawar Pyaaz Tamatar Masala

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 2-3 days; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because gawar becomes excessively soft and watery after thawing  
**Active time:** 30-35 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: semi-dry, lightly tangy gawar sabji. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient        |                                      Quantity |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------: |
| Gawar fali        |                         400 g before trimming |
| Onion             | 1 medium, about 100 g, finely chopped |
| Tomatoes          | 2 medium, about 180-200 g, finely chopped |
| Garlic            |                  4 cloves, finely chopped |
| Green chilli      |                  1-2, finely chopped |
| Oil               |                                    1½ tbsp |
| Jeera             |                                      ½ tsp |
| Hing              |                                    1 pinch |
| Haldi             |                                      ¼ tsp |
| Red chilli powder |                  ½ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Dhania powder     |                                       1 tsp |
| Salt              |             ¾ tsp, divided; adjust to taste |
| Amchur            |                                      ½ tsp |
| Garam masala      |                            ¼ tsp, optional |
| Water             |                     ¼ cup, about 60 ml |

## Process

1. Wash the gawar fali thoroughly and drain it. Remove the stalk end and any tough strings running along the sides. Chop into approximately **2 cm pieces**.
2. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in a kadai over **medium heat**.
3. Add **½ tsp jeera**. Once it crackles, add **1 pinch hing**.
4. Add the chopped onion and approximately **¼ tsp salt**. Cook over **medium heat for 5-6 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until the onion becomes soft and lightly golden.
5. Add the chopped garlic and green chilli. Sauté for **1 minute** until the raw garlic smell reduces.
6. Add the chopped tomatoes, **¼ tsp haldi**, **½ tsp red chilli powder**, **1 tsp dhania powder**, and the remaining **½ tsp salt**.
7. Cook over **medium heat for 6-8 minutes**, stirring and lightly mashing the tomatoes, until they become soft and the masala starts leaving oil at the edges.
8. Add the chopped gawar. Mix thoroughly and sauté with the masala over **medium heat for 3-4 minutes**.
9. Add **¼ cup water** and mix. Cover the kadai and cook over **low-medium heat for 15-18 minutes**.
10. Open and stir every **4-5 minutes** so the sabji does not stick. Add only **1-2 tbsp extra water** if the kadai becomes completely dry before the gawar is tender.
11. Check a thicker piece of gawar. It should be completely cooked and tender but should still retain its shape.
12. Remove the lid and cook over **medium heat for 3-5 minutes**, stirring regularly, until excess moisture evaporates and the sabji becomes semi-dry.
13. Add **½ tsp amchur** and **¼ tsp garam masala**, if using. Mix and cook for another **1 minute**. Taste and adjust the salt.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**, one each for lunch and dinner.
- Each portion is suitable with **3 rotis**.
- After cooking, remove the dinner portion from the hot kadai and allow it to cool for **30-40 minutes**.
- Transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate immediately. Do not leave it at room temperature until evening.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten in a kadai with **1-2 tbsp water**.
- Cover and heat over **low-medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, stirring once or twice, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not freeze this sabji.
