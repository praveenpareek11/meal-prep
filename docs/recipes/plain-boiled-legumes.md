# Plain-Boiled Legumes — Rajma / Chole / Chana / Moth

**Type:** weekend-prep  
**Fridge life:** 3 days · **Freezer-safe:** yes (2-3 weeks)  
**Active time:** ~30 min per batch (passive cooker time) · **Makes:** 1 cup dry = 3 meals  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own, validated  

*Boil plain on the weekend; add masala fresh on the day. Plain-boiled = flexible.*

**Batch size:** 1 cup dry per variety = ~2.5 cups boiled = 3 meals. Boil 2 varieties per week.

## Method

1. Saturday night (or 8-12 hrs before prep): soak each variety separately in plenty of water. Keep the bowl loosely covered, not airtight.
2. Prep day: drain, rinse well, and add to the pressure cooker with fresh water, ½ tsp salt, and ¼ tsp haldi.
   - Rajma: 1 cup dry rajma (measured before soaking) + 3 cups water → 5-6 whistles, then low flame 10 min
   - Chole/chana: 1 cup dry chole/chana (measured before soaking) + 3 cups water → 5-6 whistles
   - Moth/moong (whole): 1 cup dry moth/moong (measured before soaking) + 2.5 cups water → 2-3 whistles
3. Let pressure release naturally.
4. Check doneness: rajma/chole/chana should mash easily between fingers but still hold shape. If firm, pressure cook 1-2 whistles more.
5. Cool in the cooker. **Store with the cooking liquid** — it keeps them moist and the liquid enriches the gravy later.

## Portion & Store

- First 3 days' portions → fridge (glass box)
- Rest → freezer with liquid, flat containers. Keeps 2-3 weeks.

**On the day (10-12 min):** heat 1 portion masala base in kadai → add boiled legumes with their liquid → garam masala / chole masala / amchur as suits → simmer 8-10 min → done. Same boiled chana also works as a quick chaat (see Variation 3).
