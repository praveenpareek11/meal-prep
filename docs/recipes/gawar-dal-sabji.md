# Gawar Dal Sabji

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 2-3 days; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because cooked gawar loses texture after freezing  
**Active time:** 35-40 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: semi-dry gawar sabji with lightly spiced dal. Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Gawar fali | 350-400 g before trimming |
| Yellow moong dal | ⅓ cup, about 65-70 g |
| Water for soaking | Enough to cover the dal |
| Onion | 1 small, about 70-80 g, finely chopped |
| Garlic | 4 cloves, finely chopped |
| Green chilli | 1-2, finely chopped |
| Oil | 1½ tbsp |
| Jeera | ½ tsp |
| Hing | 1 generous pinch |
| Haldi | ¼ tsp |
| Red chilli powder | ½ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Dhania powder | 1 tsp |
| Salt | ¾ tsp, or to taste |
| Water for cooking | ½ cup, about 120 ml |
| Amchur | ½ tsp |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp, optional |

## Process

1. Rinse **⅓ cup yellow moong dal** until the water runs mostly clear. Soak it in enough water for **20-30 minutes**, then drain.
2. Wash the gawar thoroughly. Remove the stalk ends and pull away any tough strings from the sides.
3. Chop the gawar into approximately **2 cm pieces**.
4. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in a kadai over **medium heat**.
5. Add **½ tsp jeera**. Once it crackles, add **1 generous pinch hing**.
6. Add the chopped onion and cook over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, until soft and lightly golden.
7. Add the chopped garlic and green chilli. Sauté for **1 minute**.
8. Reduce the heat to **low**. Add **¼ tsp haldi**, **½ tsp red chilli powder**, and **1 tsp dhania powder**. Stir for **10-15 seconds**.
9. Immediately add the chopped gawar and **¾ tsp salt**. Mix well and sauté over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**.
10. Add the drained moong dal and mix gently with the gawar.
11. Add **½ cup water**. Cover and cook over **low-medium heat for 18-22 minutes**.
12. Open and stir gently every **5 minutes**. Do not stir aggressively, otherwise the moong dal may turn mushy.
13. If the mixture becomes completely dry before the gawar and dal are cooked, add **1-2 tbsp hot water** at a time.
14. Check the gawar and dal. The gawar should be tender, and the dal should be cooked through but still retain some shape.
15. Remove the lid and cook over **medium heat for 3-5 minutes**, stirring gently, until excess moisture evaporates and the sabji becomes semi-dry.
16. Add **½ tsp amchur** and optional **¼ tsp garam masala**.
17. Mix gently and cook for another **1 minute**. Taste and adjust the salt or amchur.

## Chana Dal Variation

- Replace yellow moong dal with **⅓ cup chana dal**.
- Soak the chana dal for **1½-2 hours**.
- Pressure cook the soaked and drained chana dal with **¾ cup water** and a pinch of haldi for **1 whistle on medium heat**.
- Allow the pressure to release naturally for **5 minutes**, then release any remaining pressure.
- The dal should be about **80% cooked** and still hold its shape.
- Add the partially cooked chana dal to the kadai after sautéing the gawar.
- Add only **¼ cup of the chana-dal cooking water**.
- Cover and cook the gawar and chana dal together over **low-medium heat for 15-18 minutes**.
- Continue with the amchur and garam masala finishing steps.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions**, one each for lunch and dinner.
- Each portion pairs with **3 rotis**.
- Remove the dinner portion from the hot kadai and let it cool for **30-40 minutes**.
- Transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate immediately.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten in a kadai with **1-2 tbsp water**.
- Cover and reheat over **low-medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, stirring once or twice, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not freeze this sabji.
