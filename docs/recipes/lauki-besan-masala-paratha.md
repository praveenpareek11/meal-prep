# Lauki Besan Masala Paratha — Bread Maker Dough

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 1 day; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because cooked parathas are not freezer-approved in the storage guide  
**Active time:** 45-55 min (morning) · **Makes:** 6-8 medium parathas, 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: soft mixed-dough lauki parathas with besan and dry masala. Serve 3-4 parathas per meal with curd or chutney.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Lauki pieces | 500-600 g, refrigerated from the previous evening |
| Atta | 250 g initially, about 2 cups |
| Extra atta | 30-80 g, as required during kneading and rolling |
| Besan | 50 g, about ½ cup |
| Green chillies | 2, roughly chopped |
| Ginger | 1-inch piece, optional |
| Ajwain | ½ tsp, lightly crushed |
| Jeera | ½ tsp, lightly crushed |
| Dhania powder | 1 tsp |
| Haldi | ½ tsp |
| Red chilli powder | ¼-½ tsp |
| Amchur | ¾ tsp |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp |
| Salt | 1¼ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Oil | 2 tsp, for the dough |
| Oil or ghee | 2-3 tbsp total, for cooking |

## Process

1. Check that the refrigerated lauki pieces are not slimy, sour-smelling or unusually discoloured. Taste a tiny raw piece; if it tastes distinctly bitter, discard the entire batch.
2. Put the raw lauki pieces, green chillies and optional ginger in the mixer-grinder. Grind in **2-3 batches**, using short pulses and **without adding water**, to make a coarse wet paste or fine mince rather than a completely liquid puree.
3. Transfer the ground lauki to a sieve or clean cloth. Press firmly enough to remove the freely dripping liquid, but do not squeeze it completely dry. Reserve the extracted lauki liquid. The target is approximately **250-300 g moist lauki pulp**, although the yield will vary.
4. Add the ground lauki pulp, **2 tsp oil**, ajwain, jeera, dhania powder, haldi, red chilli powder, amchur and garam masala to the bread-maker pan. Add **250 g atta**, **50 g besan** and **1¼ tsp salt**. Start kneading immediately after adding the salt.
5. Select only the bread maker's **Dough or Knead mode**. Do not run a complete bread-baking programme.
6. Inspect the dough after **3-4 minutes**. If it is wet and sticking heavily to the pan, add extra atta **1 tbsp at a time**, waiting **45-60 seconds** after each addition. If dry flour remains and the dough cannot come together, add the reserved lauki liquid **1 tsp at a time**. Do not add normal water unless the reserved liquid is insufficient.
7. Continue kneading for approximately **8-10 minutes**, until the dough is soft, smooth and slightly softer than regular roti dough, but not wet or paste-like. Stop the machine after kneading; do not allow a long yeast-style proofing cycle.
8. Remove the dough from the bread-maker pan, cover it and rest for only **10 minutes**. Lauki and salt continue releasing moisture, so do not leave the dough resting for much longer.
9. Lightly knead the rested dough by hand. If it has become sticky, knead in **1-2 tbsp atta**. Divide it into **6 balls for larger parathas** or **7-8 balls for medium parathas**.
10. Dust one ball lightly with atta and roll it into a circle approximately **15-17 cm wide**, slightly thicker than a regular roti. Avoid excessive dry flour because it can make the paratha dry.
11. Heat a tawa over **medium-high heat**. Place one rolled paratha on the hot tawa and cook the first side for **35-45 seconds**, until small bubbles appear.
12. Flip and cook for **30-40 seconds**. Apply approximately **½-¾ tsp oil or ghee** around the edges and over the surface. Flip again and cook over **medium heat for 30-45 seconds**, pressing lightly with a spatula, until both sides have golden-brown spots and the centre is fully cooked.
13. Repeat with the remaining dough balls. Reduce the heat slightly if the tawa becomes too hot and the surface browns before the centre cooks.

## Portioning and Storage

- Serve **3-4 parathas for lunch** with home curd.
- Keep the dinner portion separate so it is not repeatedly handled.
- Cool the dinner parathas to warm within **30-40 minutes**, then transfer them to an airtight container and refrigerate immediately.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten on a covered tawa over **low-medium heat for 1-2 minutes per side**, until piping hot throughout.
- Serve the dinner portion with chutney.
- Do not refrigerate or reheat the same portion again.
- Cook the complete batch in the morning. If any raw dough remains, refrigerate it and use it within **1 day maximum**; do not freeze the cooked parathas.
