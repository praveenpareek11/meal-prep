# Aloo-Pyaaz-Tamatar Jhol Wali Sabji

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 1 day; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because potato texture deteriorates after freezing  
**Active time:** 20-25 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  
**Kitchen-tested:** ❌ not yet  

*Style: thin, spoonable jhol gravy — the runny cousin of the semi-dry [Aloo Pyaaz Tamatar Sabzi](aloo-pyaaz-tamatar-sabzi.md). Pairs with 3 rotis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient        |                           Quantity |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------: |
| Potato            |          3 medium, about 300-350 g |
| Onion             |           1 medium, finely chopped |
| Tomato            |           2 medium, finely chopped |
| Garlic            | 5-6 cloves, finely chopped/crushed |
| Ginger            |               ½-inch piece, grated |
| Green chilli      |             1, finely chopped/slit |
| Oil               |                            1½ tbsp |
| Jeera             |                              ½ tsp |
| Hing              |                            1 pinch |
| Haldi             |                              ½ tsp |
| Red chilli powder |                            ¼-½ tsp |
| Dhania powder     |                             1½ tsp |
| Jeera powder      |                              ½ tsp |
| Salt              |                 1 tsp, or to taste |
| Garam masala      |                              ¼ tsp |
| Amchur            |                    ¼ tsp, optional |
| Water             |                            2½ cups |
| Kasuri methi      |                    ½ tsp, optional |
| Fresh coriander   |                   1 tbsp, optional |

## Process — Pressure Cooker

1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into approximately 1-inch pieces. Keep the pieces medium-sized so they do not completely dissolve during pressure cooking.
2. Heat **1½ tbsp oil** in the pressure cooker on medium flame.
3. Add **½ tsp jeera**. Once it crackles, add the **hing**.
4. Add the chopped onion and cook for **4-5 minutes**, until soft and lightly golden.
5. Add garlic, ginger and green chilli. Cook for **1 minute**, until the raw garlic smell reduces.
6. Add the tomatoes, **½ tsp salt**, haldi, red chilli powder, dhania powder and jeera powder.
7. Cook for **5-6 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes become soft and the masala begins leaving oil at the edges.
8. Add the chopped potatoes and cook them with the masala for **2 minutes**.
9. Add **2½ cups water** and the remaining salt. Mix well and scrape any masala stuck to the bottom.
10. Close the pressure cooker and cook on medium flame for **2 whistles**.
11. Turn off the flame and allow the pressure to release naturally for approximately **8-10 minutes**.
12. Open the cooker and lightly mash **3-4 potato pieces** against the side of the cooker. This will slightly thicken the gravy while keeping it sufficiently liquid.
13. Add garam masala, optional amchur and crushed kasuri methi.
14. Simmer uncovered for **3-5 minutes**. Add another **¼ cup hot water** if the gravy has become thicker than desired.
15. Taste and adjust the salt. Add coriander, if using.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the sabji into **2 equal portions** immediately after cooking.
- Keep the lunch portion covered for serving.
- Let the dinner portion cool for **30-40 minutes**, then transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate.
- At dinner, reheat only that portion in a small kadai on low-medium flame with **2-3 tbsp water**, stirring occasionally, until piping hot.
