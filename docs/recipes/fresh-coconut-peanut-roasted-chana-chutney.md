# Fresh Coconut, Peanut and Roasted Chana Chutney

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** Same day; keep refrigerated and consume the dinner portion by evening · **Freezer-safe:** No, because fresh coconut chutney loses its texture and fresh flavour after freezing  
**Active time:** 10-12 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 side portions for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: thick, coconut-forward South Indian chutney for about 4 idlis per meal.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Fresh mature coconut flesh | 70-80 g, about ¾ cup chopped |
| Roasted chana dal / dalia / putani, without peel | 1 tbsp |
| Peanuts | 2 tsp |
| Green chilli | 1 small, adjust to taste |
| Ginger | ½-inch piece |
| Garlic | 1 small clove |
| Curry leaves | 4-5 leaves |
| Salt | ⅓ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Water | ¼-⅓ cup, as needed |
| Lemon juice | ½ tsp, optional |

### Optional Tempering

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Oil | 1 tsp |
| Mustard seeds | ¼ tsp |
| Urad dal | ¼ tsp, optional |
| Curry leaves | 4-5 leaves |
| Hing | 1 small pinch |

## Process

1. Remove approximately **70-80 g coconut flesh** and cut it into small pieces.
2. Dry-roast **2 tsp peanuts** over **low heat for 3-4 minutes**, until lightly coloured and aromatic. Let them cool completely.
3. Add the coconut, roasted peanuts, **1 tbsp roasted chana dal**, **1 small green chilli**, **½-inch ginger**, **1 small garlic clove**, **4-5 curry leaves**, and **⅓ tsp salt** to the mixer jar.
4. Add only **¼ cup water** initially.
5. Grind using short pulses, scraping down the sides as required, until reasonably smooth.
6. Check the consistency. Add more water only **1 tbsp at a time**. Keep the total water between approximately **¼ and ⅓ cup**.
7. Taste and adjust the salt. Mix in **½ tsp lemon juice**, if using.
8. For the optional tempering, heat **1 tsp oil** over **medium heat**.
9. Add **¼ tsp mustard seeds**. Once they splutter, add **¼ tsp urad dal**, **4-5 curry leaves**, and a small pinch of hing.
10. Cook for **20-30 seconds**, switch off the heat, and pour the tempering over the chutney.

## Portioning and Storage

- This quantity makes approximately **¾ cup thick chutney**, sufficient for two idli meals.
- Divide the chutney into **2 separate portions** after preparation.
- Keep both portions refrigerated if lunch is not being eaten immediately.
- Do not leave the chutney at room temperature for more than **30-40 minutes** before covering and refrigerating it.
- Remove only the portion being served; keep the dinner portion refrigerated.
- Consume the refrigerated dinner portion by the same evening.
- Do not reheat coconut chutney. Let the required portion sit outside for **10-15 minutes** before serving if you do not want it fridge-cold.
- Store unused coconut pieces separately in a clean, dry, airtight container. Do not store them submerged in coconut water.
- Do not freeze the chutney.
