# Steamed Idli

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** Same day; refrigerate the dinner portion after cooling 30-40 min · **Freezer-safe:** No, because freezing is outside the current storage plan and can make reheated idlis dry  
**Active time:** 15-20 min (morning) · **Makes:** 2 meals for 1 person, about 8 medium idlis  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: soft steamed idlis. Serve about 4 idlis per meal with sambar/dal, coconut chutney, or podi.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Readymade idli/dosa batter | 450-500 g, about 2 cups |
| Salt | ⅛-¼ tsp, only if the batter needs it |
| Oil | ½ tsp, for greasing the moulds |
| Water | 2-2½ cups, for steaming |

## Process

1. Remove **450-500 g batter** from the refrigerator and mix it gently for **15-20 seconds**. Do not beat vigorously.
2. Check the consistency. The batter should be thick but pourable. If it is unusually thick, mix in **1 tbsp water at a time**, using no more than **2 tbsp** unless required.
3. Taste a tiny amount and add **⅛-¼ tsp salt** only if the readymade batter needs it.
4. Add **2-2½ cups water** to the idli pot. Keep the water level below the lowest idli tray.
5. Cover and bring the water to a strong boil over **high heat for 4-5 minutes**.
6. Meanwhile, grease **8 idli moulds** lightly with approximately **½ tsp oil** in total.
7. Fill each mould approximately **¾ full** with batter. Do not fill to the rim.
8. Once the water is boiling, place the filled trays in the pot and cover immediately.
9. Steam over **medium-high heat for 10-12 minutes**. Do not open the lid during the first **10 minutes**.
10. Insert a toothpick or knife into the centre of one idli. If wet batter sticks to it, steam for another **2 minutes**.
11. Switch off the heat and leave the pot covered for **2 minutes**.
12. Remove the trays and rest them for **3-4 minutes**. Loosen each idli with a wet spoon or knife and remove gently.

## Portioning and Storage

- Divide the idlis into **2 portions of about 4 idlis each**, one for lunch and one for dinner.
- Remove the dinner portion from the hot tray and let it cool for **30-40 minutes**.
- Transfer it to a covered container and refrigerate immediately.
- Store chutney, sambar, or dal separately according to its own storage rules.
- At dinner, reheat only the portion being eaten.
- To reheat, place the idlis on a greased steel plate or steaming tray above a small amount of boiling water.
- Cover and steam over **medium heat for 4-5 minutes**, until piping hot throughout.
- Do not repeatedly cool and reheat the same idlis.
- Do not freeze the cooked idlis.
