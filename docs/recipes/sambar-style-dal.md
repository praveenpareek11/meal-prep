# Sambar-Style Dal (the Idli Companion)

**Type:** weekend-prep  
**Fridge life:** 3 days · **Freezer-safe:** yes (2 weeks)  
**Active time:** 25 min · **Makes:** 4 portions  
**Uses prepped components:** chopped veg box or frozen veg  
**Source:** own, validated  

*80% of sambar at 30% of the effort.*

**Batch:** 1 cup toor dal version = 4 portions | **Time:** 25 min

1. Rinse 1 cup toor dal well. Pressure cook with 3 cups water, ¼ tsp haldi, ½ tsp oil, chopped lauki or frozen carrot/beans, and 1 chopped tomato — 4 whistles. Let pressure release naturally.
2. Open and mash the dal lightly. Add 1.5-2 tbsp sambar powder, small lemon-sized tamarind soaked and squeezed (or 2 tsp tamarind paste), salt, jaggery pinch, and 1-1.5 cups water as needed. Simmer 8-10 min.
3. Tadka: heat 2 tsp oil, add ½ tsp mustard seeds, hing, curry leaves, and 1 dry red chilli. Pour into the simmered sambar-style dal and cover 2 min.

**Store:** 2 portions fridge, 2 freezer. Idli day = steam idlis from readymade batter (12-15 min, passive) + reheat this = a real 15-minute meal. Batter keeps 3-4 days in fridge; buy it fresh with your weekly shop.
