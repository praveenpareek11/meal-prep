# One-Pot Thin Dal Sambar for Idli

**Type:** weekday-dish  
**Fridge life:** 3 days · **Freezer-safe:** Yes, up to 2 weeks  
**Active time:** 12-15 min · **Makes:** 325-350 ml; 1 dinner for 1 person  
**Uses prepped components:** none  
**Source:** own  

*Style: thin, pourable and mildly tangy sambar for 6 idlis, cooked entirely in one pressure cooker.*

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | ---: |
| Arhar dal | 1½ tbsp, about 20 g |
| Moong dhuli dal | 1 tbsp, about 13 g |
| Moong chhilka dal | 1 tsp, about 5 g |
| Water | 250 ml for pressure cooking + 75-100 ml later |
| Oil | 1 tsp |
| Rai | ½ tsp |
| Jeera | ¼ tsp |
| Hing | 1 pinch |
| Dry red chilli | 1, broken, optional |
| Curry leaves | 5-6, optional but recommended |
| Haldi | ⅛ tsp |
| Sambar masala | 1¼ tsp |
| Tamarind paste | ½ tsp |
| Salt | ½ tsp, adjust to taste |
| Jaggery or sugar | ⅛ tsp, optional |

## Process — One-Pot Pressure Cooker

1. Wash all three dals together **2-3 times**, then drain.
2. Heat **1 tsp oil** in the open pressure cooker over **medium-low heat**.
3. Add **½ tsp rai**. Once it crackles, add **¼ tsp jeera**, **1 pinch hing**, the optional dry red chilli and **5-6 curry leaves**.
4. Fry for **20-30 seconds** without letting the jeera, hing or curry leaves burn.
5. Add the washed dals and **⅛ tsp haldi**. Stir for **20-30 seconds** to coat the dals with the tempering.
6. Add **250 ml water** and scrape the bottom thoroughly so nothing remains stuck.
7. Close the cooker and cook over **medium-high heat until the first whistle**. Reduce to **low heat** and cook for another **6 minutes**.
8. Switch off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally.
9. Open the cooker and mash the cooked dals thoroughly with a spoon or whisk.
10. Add **½ tsp tamarind paste**, **1¼ tsp sambar masala**, **½ tsp salt** and the optional **⅛ tsp jaggery or sugar**. Do not add the tamarind before pressure cooking because its acidity can slow the softening of the dal.
11. Add **75 ml hot water**, mix well and simmer uncovered in the same cooker over **medium-low heat for 6-8 minutes**, stirring occasionally.
12. Add up to **25 ml more hot water** if required. The finished sambar should be thin and easily pourable, with approximately **325-350 ml** total volume.
13. Taste and adjust the salt or tamarind. Serve piping hot with **6 idlis** and coconut chutney.

## Portioning and Storage

- This quantity is intended for one dinner and can be consumed completely with the idlis.
- If storing any remainder, cool it to warm within **30-40 minutes**, cover and refrigerate.
- Refrigerated sambar keeps for up to **3 days** and is freezer-safe for up to **2 weeks**.
- Reheat only the portion being eaten in a small kadai or the pressure-cooker base.
- Add **1-2 tbsp water** if it has thickened and bring it to a bubbling simmer before serving.
