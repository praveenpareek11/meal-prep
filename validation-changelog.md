## Round 1 — Masala Base, Legumes, Dal

### 1. Onion-Tomato Masala Base
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Onion cut | sliced only → finely chopped or thinly sliced | Chopped/pulsed onion gives a smoother reusable base |
| Oil | 4-5 tbsp → 4 tbsp | Enough for the batch; avoids oily base |
| Jeera added | none → 1 tsp jeera tempering | Improves base flavour without over-specificity |
| Spice technique | powders added with tomatoes → bloomed with 2-3 tbsp water before tomatoes | Prevents powder spices burning in a big batch |
| Salt line | 1 tsp salt → 1 tsp salt, divided | Keeps browning benefit, avoids over-salting |

### 2. Plain-Boiled Legumes
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Soak time | 8 hrs → 8-12 hrs | Faster cooking, better digestibility |
| Soak covering | unspecified → loosely covered, not airtight | Prevents sweaty soaking in humidity |
| Water quantity | "2 inches above" → exact cups per legume | Repeatable batches |
| Moth/moong timing | 3-4 whistles → 2-3 whistles | They cook faster; avoid mush |
| Doneness check | none → mash-between-fingers + extra whistles if firm | Older beans need more cooking |

**Owner fix applied:** validator wrote "1 cup soaked + 3 cups water" which conflicted with the "1 cup dry" batch size. Corrected to "1 cup dry (measured before soaking) + 3 cups water" (rajma/chole) and "+ 2.5 cups" (moth/moong).

### 3. Plain-Boiled Dal + Fresh Tadka
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Rinsing | rinse → rinse 3-4 times till mostly clear | Less starch/foam |
| Oil in cooker | none → ½ tsp oil/ghee | Reduces foaming |
| Timing split | both 3-4 whistles → toor 3-4, moong 2-3 | Moong overcooks easily |
| Warm whisk | none → whisk once while warm | Prevents lumpy dal after cooling |
| Tadka quantity | 1 tsp vague → 2 tsp ghee for lunch+dinner day | Matches portion basis |
| Reheat method | pour tadka over dal → tadka + dal + water simmer 3-4 min | Integrated taste, not just reheated |

## Round 2 — Potatoes, Veggie Boxes, Curd

### 4. Boiled Potatoes
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Washing | unspecified → wash well before boiling | Skin-on storage needs clean skin |
| Water | unspecified → 1 cup in cooker | Repeatable |
| Size adjustment | 3 whistles → 3 medium / 4 very large + knife check | Size changes cook time |
| Release | unspecified → natural release | Prevents split/watery potatoes |
| Drying | cool fully → cool on plate/colander till surface dry | Less condensation in dabba (monsoon) |

### 5. Chopped Veggie Boxes
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Wash timing | wash, dry, chop → wash BEFORE chopping, dry, then chop | Less water trapped in cut veg |
| Fridge placement | unspecified → cold shelf, not door | Door temperature fluctuates |
| Lauki/toru/kaddu note | generic → peel, chop, dry again if watery | Watery veg release moisture after cutting |
| Gobi note | keep dry → rinse/inspect, then dry thoroughly | Needs inspection before storage |
| Onion note | sealed box only → chop dry; do NOT wash after chopping | Washed chopped onion spoils faster |

**FLAG (accepted):** 3-4 days for cut veg is the upper edge in monsoon. Ruling: keep written claim; plan menus to consume chopped veg by day 3, day 4 = smell-test fallback. → Add one line to storage cheat sheet during merge.

### 6. Home Curd
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Simmer | boil → boil then simmer 2-3 min | Thicker curd |
| Starter quantity | 1 tbsp → 1-2 tsp | Excess starter in warm humidity = sour by day 2 |
| Mixing technique | whisk into milk → dilute starter in 2 tbsp warm milk first | Even culture distribution, no lumps |
| Setting time | overnight → 6-8 hrs or overnight, check earlier in warm weather | Warm conditions speed souring |

## Round 3 — Sprouts, Rotis/Theplas, Sambar-Dal, Freezer Guide

### 7. Monsoon-Safe Sprouts
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cleaning | soak directly → pick/rinse first, then soak | Dirt/broken grains spoil faster |
| Method | damp cloth or colander → covered colander; avoid wet cloth | Excess wetness = bacterial risk in monsoon |
| Rinse detail | rinse 2x/day → rinse AND drain fully | Full draining reduces sliminess |
| Spoilage check | none → discard if sticky/sour/slimy | Sprouts are high-risk |
| Cooking time | steam 5-7 min → steam 8-10 min (sauté 7-8 covered) | Thorough cooking per food-safety guidance |
| Curd timing | mixed directly → add only after sprouts cool | Prevents splitting/souring |
| Protein claim | "~18-20g" → depends on curd quantity | Original only true with generous curd (½ cup dry moong ≈ 12g + curd 4-6g) |

### 8. Batch Rotis / Theplas
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Roti quantities | none → 210-240g atta + 130-150ml water for 6 | Repeatable 2-meal batch |
| Resting | none → rest dough 10-15 min | Softness and rolling |
| Ghee timing | smear while cooking → smear AFTER cooking | Doesn't interfere with puffing |
| Cooling | cool fully → 5-8 min so steam escapes, then box | Prevents soggy airtight storage |
| Thepla ratio | "besan ¼ of atta" → exact 180g atta + 45g besan + ¼ cup curd + 1 tbsp oil | Usable quantities; too much curd/water shortens shelf life |
| Layering | stack → stack with paper/cloth between layers | Prevents sticking and moisture buildup |

**FLAG (accepted):** "taste fine at room temp" — fridge is the default in monsoon; room-temp property applies to office-day tiffin use only. → Reword during merge.

### 9. Sambar-Style Dal
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Rinse | unspecified → rinse well | Cleaner taste, less foam |
| Water | unspecified → 3 cups cooking + 1-1.5 cups at simmer | Prevents over-thick dal |
| Oil in cooker | none → ½ tsp | Reduces foaming |
| Release | unspecified → natural release | Even, safe finish |
| Mash step | none → mash lightly before adding sambar powder/tamarind | Sambar needs soft dal body |
| Tadka quantities | ingredients only → 2 tsp oil, ½ tsp mustard | Repeatable |
| Cover after tadka | none → cover 2 min | Traps aroma before storage |

### 10. Freezer Portioning Guide
| What changed | From → To | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cooling instruction | unspecified → cool till steam stops (warm, not hot), don't leave out hours | Safe-time-limit compliance |
| Headspace | none → leave headspace for liquid foods | Frozen liquids expand |
| Frozen-block reheat | into kadai → low flame + water splash + reheat piping hot | Prevents scorched-outside/cold-center |
| Microwave note | "defrost 2-3 min, done" → defrost THEN reheat fully hot | Defrosting ≠ reheating |
